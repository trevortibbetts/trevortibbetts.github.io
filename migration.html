Mapping Creative Mobility and Urban Cultural Hubs

Analyzing the national migration networks of specialized creative talent through spatial data visualization.

Tools: Python, GIS, Spatial Network Analysis, p5.js • Theme: Urban Geography & Cultural Economies

Project Overview

This project applies spatial network visualization to analyze internal migration patterns among entertainment professionals featured on national media platforms[cite: 1]. The resulting map traces individual trajectories from hometown origins to primary career destinations, mapping out intricate networks of professional relocation[cite: 1].

Significance

While classical urban economics frameworks traditionally evaluate global command centers through corporate and financial lenses, this research investigates how specialized cultural economies mirror those exact spatial agglomeration trends[cite: 1]. It examines how creative industries cluster in specific metropolitan nodes to optimize visibility, collaboration, and career development[cite: 1].

Analytical Findings

The spatial data highlights prominent metropolitan hubs—such as Los Angeles, Las Vegas, Chicago, and New York—as major centers for specialized talent[cite: 1]. Specifically, Los Angeles emerges as a primary focal point influenced by regional media infrastructure, while Las Vegas demonstrates a strong structural pull as a live-entertainment center[cite: 1].

Interactive network visualization of migration pathways. Data was collected via API requests from the RuPaul’s Drag Race fandom page, processed into spatial geometries, and structured into a GeoDataFrame for spatial analysis.

Possible Extensions

Future development will focus on optimizing data hygiene and geometry processing to streamline dense network flows, reduce visual overlap, and enhance overall cartographic clarity.
